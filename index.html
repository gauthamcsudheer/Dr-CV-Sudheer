About Me

I'm Dr. C.V. Sudheer — a dedicated Language Editor, Researcher, and Assistant Professor in Malayalam with over 20 years of experience in academia and editorial work. My journey has been rooted in a deep commitment to Malayalam language and literature, spanning teaching, research, editing, and publication.

With a Ph.D. in Malayalam and multiple prestigious awards including the Kerala Sahitya Akademi Award and the Dr. Suvarna Nalappat Endowment, I've consistently contributed to scholarly discourse. My editorial work has enhanced the publication potential of numerous academic papers, while my seminars and presentations—both national and international—have explored everything from grammar formalism to Jewish Malayalam folk narratives.

Whether I'm editing a manuscript for clarity and precision, presenting at a folklore seminar, or mentoring students, my goal remains the same: to contribute meaningfully to the world of Malayalam studies through rigorous scholarship, language refinement, and passionate teaching.

Let's connect and collaborate on ideas that matter.

Research Interests

Malayalam Grammar and Linguistic Theory

In-depth exploration of Malayalam grammar with a special focus on the contributions of A.R. Raja Raja Varma. Research includes the transition from traditional grammatical frameworks to modern syntactic theories, as well as their application in contemporary usage.

Comparative and Applied Linguistics

Linguistic analysis of Malayalam in comparison with other Dravidian languages. Special emphasis on sociolinguistics, applied linguistics, and the influence of cultural and regional factors on language development.

Folklore and Cultural Narratives

Study of folk traditions, oral narratives, and performance literature within Kerala. Research includes Jewish Malayalam songs, regional folk expressions, and their role in cultural identity.

Onomastics

Investigation into the significance of naming conventions in Malayalam literature and society. Current work involves exploring linguistic patterns in personal and place names and their socio-cultural implications.

Media and Language in the Digital Age

Analysis of the evolving usage of Malayalam in digital platforms, blogs, and e-media. Projects examine how technology influences language formality, grammar, and regional dialects online.

Ongoing Projects

Current research includes documenting regional variations in Malayalam grammar, developing linguistic resources for academic use, and editing critical anthologies in Malayalam language studies.
